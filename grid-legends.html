Nuts and Bolts

Whenever I'd tell friends what I'm working on at Feral, I'd always get the same question: but GRID's already been made? Unfortunately (or fortunately, if you happen to be on a porting house's payroll) it's not so simple. One of the two major obstacles in shipping an AAA title to mobile is optimising its graphics, which is more or less the art of Running The Game At 60FPS Without It Burning Your Fingerprints Off. The other is the UI.

As a generalist, one of my core responsibilities in the year-and-change I spent on GRID was helping overhaul its user interface. From the HUD to the frontend to pause menu, pretty much every screen in the game needed tailoring to the handheld experience... which takes a lot of dev time, and causes a lot of regressions. This being my first commercial project, I hadn't realised how much skill there is to navigating a knotty codebase, day in, day out. As soon as I found my bearings, [...] - more often than not jury-rigging Codemasters' UI tools to do things they were never meant to!
